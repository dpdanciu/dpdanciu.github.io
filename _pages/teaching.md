### Teaching, Mentoring & Academic Leadership  

Bridging interdisciplinary gaps requires active mentorship and education. My academic teaching and leadership experience ranges from core undergraduate mathematics courses to guiding interdisciplinary research projects. 

### Student Mentoring & Co-Supervision  

Heidelberg University (2017 – Present) I take an active role in training the next generation of mathematical biologists and quantitative scientists. To date, I have co-supervised and mentored:   

*	6 Bachelor of Arts (BA) Theses in Mathematics  

*	2 PhD Student Projects (Quantitative Tutoring)  

*	1 MSc Student Project  

*	1 Fulbright Visiting Scholar  

*	2 Biology Rotation Students (Guiding the transition into data-driven modeling)  


### Classroom Teaching Experience  

*	Teaching Assistant / Course Management: Mathematische Methoden in der Systembiologie (Mathematical Methods in Systems Biology), Heidelberg University (Winter Semesters 2017/2018 and 2015/2016).  

*	Undergraduate Tutor: Supervised undergraduate students in computational workflows and numerical projects using MATLAB, Murray Edwards College, University of Cambridge (2012–2014).  

*	Teaching Volunteer: Assistant via the STIMULUS program, aiding local Cambridge sixth-form and high school pupils in advanced GCSE and A-level mathematics.  


### Academic Leadership & Community Service
*	Organizing Committee Member: 12th European Conference on Mathematical and Theoretical Biology (ECMTB 2022, Heidelberg). Co-managed a major international hybrid scientific assembly welcoming 620 in-person and 100 online participants.  

*	Co-Founder: The "Mathematics of Life" Student Society under HGS MathComp, establishing an interdisciplinary platform to facilitate collaboration between theoretical and experimental groups on campus.  

*	Secretary: Heidelberg Student Chapter of the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM).
