My research centers on understanding how stem cell systems maintain long-term robustness and how the dysregulation of activation and self-renewal drives aging, regeneration failure, and malignant growth. I tailor computational and mathematical models to specific biological questions through close, interdisciplinary collaborations with experimental groups. 

### 1. Regulation of Neural Stem Cell Dynamics in Adult Neurogenesis   
**Postdoctoral Research**  
As part of the ERC Synergy Grant PErPetuating Stemness (PEPS), in collaboration with DKFZ (Heidelberg) and Institute Pasteur (Paris), I develop deterministic and stochastic models to dissect the regulatory feedback loops controlling adult neural stem cells. Using mammalian (mouse) and zebrafish data, my work performs cross-species comparisons to identify why zebrafish maintain lifelong neurogenic resilience while mammalian systems progressively decline with age.  
*	Methodologies: Nonlinear ODE systems, model selection, Bayesian uncertainty quantification, data-driven calibration. 
*	Key Insight: Characterized distinct classes of feedback motifs that act as dominant control parameters preserving long-term stem cell pools under physiological and perturbed conditions. 

### 2. From Homeostasis to Malignancy: Distorted Systems in Glioblastoma
**Postdoctoral Research**  
Within the Collaborative Research Centre SFB 1324, in collaboration with DKFZ (Heidelberg), I extend mechanistic models of neural stem cells to explore how stem-like tumor cells hijack neurodevelopmental programs and dysregulate the Wnt signalling pathway in glioblastoma brain cancer.  
*	Methodologies: Data integration with single-cell transcriptomics and time-series population dynamics.  
*	Key Insight: Demonstrated that glioblastoma expansion can be mathematically characterized as a distorted regulatory system rather than simple uncontrolled proliferation, highlighting the transition between quiescence and activation as a dominant therapeutic target.  

### 3. Wnt-Mediated Robustness in Drosophila Niches
**Postdoctoral Research**  
Within the Collaborative Research Centre SFB 1324, in collaboration with the Centre for Organismal Studies (Heidelberg), I investigate how Wnt signaling cascades govern stem cell identity and fate transitions in the male germline stem cell niche in Drosophila melanogaster.  
* Methodologies: Stochastic state-transition models, parameter optimization, and lineage tracing data integration.
* Key Insight: Characterizing the minimal regulatory motifs required to maintain niche identity under fluctuating conditions.

### 4. History-Dependent Stem Cell Regulation during Organ Growth
**Doctoral Research**  
During my PhD, within the Collaborative Research Centre SFB873, in collaboration with the Centre for Organismal Studies (Heidelberg), I investigated how stem cells coordinate post-embryonic organ growth and architectural organization using the fish gill as a model system. 
*	Methodologies: Stochastic modeling based on Markov chain theory, mechanistic inference using clonal lineage tracing datasets.  
*	Key Insight: Uncovered that the system is maintained by an organized pool of stem cells exhibiting history-dependent activation, where previously activated cells are primed to reactivate. 

### 5. Open Source Systems Biology Tool Development
**Master's Project** (EMBL-EBI Cambridge)  
I contributed to global optimization methodologies in systems biology by implementing BayesFit, a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) global parameter-fitting module. This package is fully integrated into the open-source MEIGO software suite on Bioconductor.
